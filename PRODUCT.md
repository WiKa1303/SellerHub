# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

German-speaking Amazon FBA sellers across the experience range — from part-time newcomers who still need guardrails to full-time operators running the app as a daily cockpit. Their context is operational: they open SellerHub to make a concrete call (source this product? raise this price? fix this PPC campaign? is this deadline handled?) and expect the app to get them to a confident answer, not to admire a dashboard. A single primary audience; the beginner/pro spread is a range within it, not two separate personas.

## Product Purpose

SellerHub is a German decision-and-obligations cockpit for Amazon sellers. It sits on top of the raw research and marketplace data (the Helium10-style layer) and turns it into decisions and duties a German seller can act on: product research and sourcing, PPC, cashflow, content/imagery, refunds, and a compliance/obligations calendar. Success is the seller reaching the **right decision faster** — with German compliance already handled — rather than assembling the answer by hand across several tools.

## Positioning

The German decision cockpit that turns marketplace data into confident seller decisions with local obligations built in — the layer that sits above research tools and tells you what to do next, not just what the numbers are.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, precise, calm. The app handles real money and legal obligations, so its voice is sober and exact — confident without hype, reassuring without hand-holding. It speaks plain German, states numbers honestly (including confidence and uncertainty), and never manufactures urgency. Guidance is available in place for those who want it and stays out of the way for those who don't.

## Anti-references

Not a generic SaaS dashboard — no interchangeable blue-gradient admin-template look with no point of view. Not a cluttered enterprise tool — avoid Seller-Central/SAP density, walls of cryptic tables, and absent hierarchy. Not a playful consumer app — no cartoonish, emoji-heavy, gamified startup vibe around money and compliance. And explicitly not a cheap hustle/dropship tool — none of the loud neon get-rich-quick aesthetic common in the seller-tool space.

## Design Principles

Decision over data — every screen should resolve toward a clear next action, not stop at a display of numbers.

Compliance as a feature — German obligations (tax, FBA duties, deadlines, refunds) are surfaced proactively and treated as first-class product value, never buried.

Calm under money — because real money is on the line, the interface stays sober and lowers anxiety instead of amplifying it; no false urgency, no hype.

Honest precision — accurate figures with visible confidence levels; earn trust by never overstating certainty.

Explain in place — meet the user where they are with inline guidance for newcomers, without slowing down or talking down to experienced sellers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body text at ≥4.5:1 contrast, full keyboard navigation, and respect for `prefers-reduced-motion`. Sensible defaults over a formal audit mandate, but AA is the baseline every new surface should meet.
